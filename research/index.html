Research Experience

Focused Blind Deconvolution (FBD)

  • Developed this blind deconvolution method that extracts sparse and front-loaded impulse responses from the channel outputs, i.e., their convolutions with a single arbitrary source. [paper]
  • This method resolves the indeterminacy inherent to multichannel blind deconvolution and I have demonstrated its applications in both exploration and global seismology.
  • In the seismic context, the extracted impulse responses correspond to the Green's function of the subsurface and can be directly input to the imaging algorithms.

Blind Source Separation/ Deblending

  • Demonstrated the application of frequency-domain independent component analysis to deblend random seismic sources. [slides] [paper]
  • ICA uses higher-order statistics to measure the Gaussianity of signals and working with convolutive mixtures (as in seismic exploration) is a challenging problem.

Full Waveform Inversion (FWI)

  • I have formulated an auxiliary bump functional for seismic inversion. [slides] [paper]
  • In simple words: "When the seismic waveforms are too complicated to be analyzed during inversion, a simplification of them into envelope-like bumpy waveforms can be useful."
  • The bump functional can be used to overcome cycle-skipping in FWI if used in a multi-objective inversion strategy.
  • Identified various ambiguities while parameterizing the multi-parameter inverse problem for quantitative seismic imaging. [slides] [paper]
  • Proposed seismic inversion by maximizing the correlation between the observed and modelled seismic data. [slides]

Seismic Interferometry

  • Developed the theory of supervirtual refraction interferometry. [paper]
  • Supervirtual interferometry enhances the signal-to-noise ratio of the far-offset refracted seismic energy. It can be used to detect the presence of diving waves in the first arrivals.
  • Enhanced core-diffracted arrivals in the earthquake records using the theory of supervirtual interferometry. [slides]
